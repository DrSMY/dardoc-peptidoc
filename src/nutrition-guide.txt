This guide accompanies every GLP-1 protocol. On appetite-suppressing medication you'll eat less, so **what** you eat matters more than ever — the goal is to lose fat while protecting muscle, energy, and nutrition. All food values below are per standard serving unless noted, and are chosen for foods widely available in the UAE.

## 1. Your calorie & macronutrient targets
**Calorie deficit** — Aim for a moderate deficit of **500–750 kcal/day** below your estimated needs. This produces gradual, sustainable loss of about **0.5–1 kg per week**. Very low-calorie diets are only done under direct medical supervision.

**Macronutrient split** (of your daily calories):

| Macronutrient | Target | Focus on |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Protein | 25–30% (about 1.0–1.2 g/kg ideal body weight) | Lean meats, fish, eggs, dairy, legumes |
| Carbohydrates | 40–50% | Complex, low-GI: whole grains, vegetables, legumes |
| Fats | 25–35% | Unsaturated: olive oil, nuts, avocado |
| Fibre | 25–35 g/day minimum | Vegetables, legumes, whole grains |

**Why protein is the priority on GLP-1:** rapid weight loss can strip muscle as well as fat. Anchoring every meal with protein preserves lean mass, keeps you fuller for longer, and protects your metabolism. Aim for the higher end of your protein range and pair it with resistance training 2–3×/week.

## 2. The plate method & meal pattern
Build each main plate as:

• **½ plate** — non-starchy vegetables
• **¼ plate** — lean protein
• **¼ plate** — whole-grain or starchy carbohydrate

Then: three main meals with 1–2 planned snacks • protein at every meal • whole, unprocessed foods • limit added sugar and refined carbs • eat slowly and mindfully • smaller, more frequent meals help manage nausea • **hydrate with 2–3 litres of water daily**.

## 13. UAE & Ramadan considerations
• Adapt traditional dishes: reduce oil and ghee, grill instead of fry, add more vegetables, and control rice/bread portions
• During hot months, exercise in the morning or evening, or use indoor options (malls, gyms, swimming)
• **Ramadan:** break your fast gently — start with water and a couple of dates, then a protein-forward meal; hydrate well between iftar and suhoor; keep suhoor high in protein and fibre for lasting fullness; keep activity lighter and timed around iftar. Discuss fasting with your physician, especially around dose timing.

## 14. Activity to protect your results
• **Aerobic:** at least 150 minutes/week of moderate activity (e.g. brisk walking), spread over 3+ days — walking is a great start
• **Resistance training:** 2–3 sessions/week covering all major muscle groups — this is what preserves muscle during weight loss
• **Daily movement (NEAT):** target 8,000–10,000 steps, take the stairs, walk during calls, break up sitting every hour

## 15. Quick glossary
| Term | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Caloric deficit | Eating fewer calories than you burn; a 500–750 kcal/day deficit ≈ 0.5–1 kg loss/week |
| Complete protein | Contains all 9 essential amino acids (animal proteins, quinoa) |
| Complex carbohydrate | Slow-digesting carbs giving steady energy (whole grains, legumes, starchy veg) |
| Glycemic index (GI) | How fast a food raises blood sugar; low-GI (<55) is preferred |
| Lean protein | Protein with minimal fat (skinless chicken, white fish, egg whites, legumes) |
| NEAT | Calories burned in everyday movement outside formal exercise |
| Satiety | The feeling of fullness that suppresses hunger — driven by protein and fibre |
